Each contingent is required to have a contingent lead, who will direct the assembly and general forward movement of the contingent, and wheel monitors for every vehicle.

Wheel Monitor Responsibilities

  • Awareness of the vehicle when it is moving
  • Standing an armâ€™s-length away from vehicle wheels
  • Do not allow anyone to get between them and the vehicle
  • Communicate with the driver if they need to stop or there is a situation
  • No getting off and on the vehicle when it is moving
  • No stopping along the Parade route unless the Parade comes to a natural halt

Every vehicle in the parade must have monitors.

Regular passenger vehicles need one monitor per axle, positioned on either side of the vehicle.

Large vehicles require one two monitors per axle, one on either side at the forward and rear.

Parade Day!

On the morning of the parade collect your monitor sticker from your contingent lead and introduce yourself to your fellow monitors and the driver of the vehicle.
